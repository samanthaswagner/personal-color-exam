Forget seasons for a moment. Your coloring is really four independent dials — undertone, depth, clarity, and contrast. This exam measures each one directly, the way an optician asks "better, or worse?" — then translates the result into a season only at the very end, where it belongs.

Set the conditions first — accuracy lives here

  • Daylight only. Face a window. No indoor bulbs — they fake-warm or fake-cool everything.
  • Bare face, hair back. Makeup and hair color both lie to this test.
  • Have two real items ready if you can: gold vs. silver, white vs. cream cloth, and a peach vs. a cool-rose fabric. Hold them under your chin.
  • Judge your skin, not the color. The winner makes redness recede and under-eye shadows lift. The loser leaves you grey, sallow, or blotchy.
