The Color Exam

A little tool to find your true color palette — not by picking a "season," but by measuring the four things that actually decide what flatters you: undertone, depth, clarity, and contrast.

▶︎ Try it here: https://samanthaswagner.github.io/personal-color-exam/

What it does

Most color quizzes hand you a label like "Summer" or "Winter" and hope it fits. This one works the other way around: it walks you through a few quick side-by-side comparisons — which is kinder to your skin, A or B? — and scores each trait on its own dial. Only at the end does it translate your result into the familiar season names, where they belong.

How to take it

For the most accurate read:


Do it in daylight, facing a window — indoor bulbs throw the colors off.
Bare face, hair pulled back. Makeup and hair color both skew the results.
If you can, hold up real items while you answer: gold vs. silver, white vs. cream, a peach vs. a cool-rose fabric.
Judge your skin's reaction, not the color you like best. The right one calms redness and brightens you; the wrong one leaves you washed out.


Good to know

This is a fun, practical guide — not a scientific instrument. The most reliable evidence is always how your skin actually reacts to real fabric in natural light. Treat the season label as a translation, not a verdict.
